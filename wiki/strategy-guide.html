Basic Strategy Guide

The strongest Interstellar Fleets strategy is to build one reliable self-sufficient platform first, then scale it into a fleet only after the dust, fuel, and replication loops are stable.

Phase 1: Prepare Before Launch

Do not treat Interstellar Fleets as an early shortcut. Research starts after Promethium science because the mod expects a mature Space Age factory with stable high-tier science, superconductors, quantum processors, fusion hardware, and platform logistics.

  • Stockpile quantum processors, superconductors, low density structures, fusion generators, and platform foundations.
  • Build a platform design that can power itself with low solar availability and survive long routes without constant manual supply.
  • Include storage for dust, fusion power cells, antimatter, and replicated raw materials.
  • Keep the first platform simple enough to duplicate cleanly; merge protection requires a stable layout signature.

Phase 2: Establish The Dust Economy

Interstellar dust is the main recovery and expansion resource. Install Interstellar dust collectors early and make sure the platform hub has space, because collectors produce dust every second while the fleet is active.

  • Use several collectors before adding many drives; dust income scales with collector count, fleet size, and speed.
  • Use Quantum replicators to turn dust into missing ores and Space Age raw resources.
  • Set up dust-to-fusion-cell production before relying heavily on Stellar fusion drives.
  • After Quantum replication, use the biter egg recipe as the platform-safe way to recover eggs from scratch.

Phase 3: Scale One Good Ship

Fleet merging is best after the platform layout is boring and dependable. A ship starter pack is expensive, so each merge should represent a ship design you would actually trust to run unattended.

  • Update the fleet blueprint only after the platform is stable; doing so clears partial progress by design.
  • Merge ships after buffers are full and recipes are running, not while a platform is still being rebuilt.
  • Split before major redesigns if you want to preserve operational flexibility.
  • Use fleet size to multiply throughput without forcing Factorio to simulate every duplicate ship.

Phase 4: Choose Drives Deliberately

Stellar fusion drives are the dependable baseline. Antimatter drives are a premium speed tool. The optimal strategy is not to replace every fusion drive immediately; it is to add antimatter drives when your dust and fusion-cell economy can afford the antimatter recipe.

  • Use Stellar fusion drives for early interstellar acceleration and routine boosts.
  • Add Antimatter drives when you need high drive power per platform tile and can sustain antimatter production.
  • Enable Auto boost once fusion cells or antimatter are automated; leave it off while reserving fuel for construction or recovery.
  • Research drive-efficiency technologies repeatedly to reduce fuel pressure without removing the logistics challenge.
  • Avoid chasing near-light speed too early; Lorentz scaling makes each boost more expensive as speed rises.

Phase 5: Avoid Soft-Locks

A fleet is recoverable when it has a valid hub, power, at least one dust collector, and a Quantum replicator. With those pieces, dust can become fusion cells, antimatter, biter eggs, pentapod eggs, biological inputs, promethium chunks, and core raw resources.

  • Do not remove the last dust collector unless you have a replacement in storage.
  • Do not remove the last Quantum replicator unless the platform has enough imported supplies to rebuild one.
  • Keep emergency fusion cells and antimatter in the hub before boosting deep into a route.
  • Keep enough power generation or stored fuel to run the replicator in low-solar interstellar space.

Phase 6: Move Infinite Science Onboard

The long-term optimal factory is an interstellar science platform that keeps infinite research running while the fleet travels. Use Quantum replication as the source-material layer, then build normal Factorio production chains on top of it.

  • Produce base science packs from replicated iron ore, copper ore, coal, stone, uranium ore, and oil-derived chains supported by coal and water or ice processing.
  • Produce Space Age science from replicated planet resources: scrap and holmium for electromagnetic science, calcite and tungsten ore for metallurgic science, and lithium plus ice for cryogenic science.
  • Produce agricultural science from replicated bioflux and pentapod eggs; replicate yumako and jellynut if you want the full biological processing chain instead of direct bioflux buffering.
  • Produce promethium science from replicated promethium asteroid chunks, or from rare promethium chunks exposed by Interstellar dust crushing, plus replicated biter eggs; then feed Interstellar labs directly on the fleet.
  • Use the fleet multiplier to scale science throughput after the platform is stable, not before the science bus and spoilage-sensitive inputs are under control.

Why This Is Fun

This strategy preserves Factorio's core loop: build a working production system, identify the bottleneck, automate the missing input, and scale only after the design is stable. The mod adds a late-game abstraction layer, but the optimal play still rewards readable logistics, buffers, power planning, and incremental factory improvement rather than one-click victory.

The best fleet is not the fastest possible platform. It is the platform whose dust, replication, fuel, science, and storage loops keep working while the fleet multiplier turns one good design into a larger industrial system.
